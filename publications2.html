Welcome to the Institute of Network Cultures Library. Since 1997, over 120 books have been published by the INC. Those publications are edited by the INC and focuse on analyzing and shaping the terrain of network cultures: digital publishing, alternative revenue models, online video and design, digital counter culture and much more.
Those publications are written by writers, journalists, theoricians and students.
This platform is made to browse, read and share the publications you want. Have a nice reading!

TOD 17: The Gray Zones of Creativity and Capital

Šefik Tatlić, Gordana Nikolić
Johnatan Beller, Josephine Berry Slater, Marc James Léger, Ana Vilenica, Sandi Abram, Irmgard Emmelheinz
#industry #capitalism #globalisation #new media #neoliberal #politics#industry #capitalism #globalisation #new media #neoliberal #politics#industry #capitalism #globalisation #new media #neoliberal #politics#industry #capitalism #globalisation #new media #neoliberal #politics#industry #capitalism #globalisation #new media #neoliberal #politics
What is the correlation among the creative industries, creative industry policies, new media paradigms and capitalism as colonial relations of dominance? What is the role of these industries in the prioritization of the interests of capital at the expense of those of society and how can these paradigms be criticized in the context of the actual, neoliberal, flexible regime of reproduction of capital? To what measure is this regime ‘flexible’ and to what measure it is just an extension of rigid, feudal and racial logics that underline (post)modern representational discourses? To what measure do the concepts of creativity, transparency, openness and flexibility conceal the hegemonic nature of modern hierarchies of exploitation?
